# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers create seamless, user-centered experiences by collaborating with Product Management and engineering teams. They ensure usability, accessibility, and visual consistency throughout the project lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Design user interfaces and user flows aligned with product goals
- Conduct user research and usability testing to inform design decisions
- Collaborate on acceptance criteria related to usability and user experience
- Document design specifications and design systems
- Provide design review and feedback during development
- Ensure accessibility standards are met (WCAG compliance)

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Manager:** Refine user personas, validate design direction against market feedback
- **Developers:** Partner on implementation feasibility and design-to-code handoff
- **Project Manager:** Include design milestones and UX reviews in project timeline
- **Technical Writer:** Collaborate on user documentation and help content

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible user experiences
- Reduce user friction and support burden
- Build products that delight users and align with business objectives

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and critique sessions
- Figma or design tool documentation
- User research findings and insights
- Accessibility audit results

---

## Technical Writer

### Role Summary
Technical Writers create clear, accurate documentation that helps users understand and efficiently use products. They work across teams to capture knowledge and reduce siloed expertise.

### Responsibilities
- Write user guides, API documentation, and release notes
- Document new features and process changes
- Create internal handbooks and onboarding materials
- Maintain documentation standards and templates
- Collaborate with teams to ensure clarity of project artifacts
- Support knowledge transfer and reduce single-person dependency

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers:** Understand technical implementation to document features accurately
- **Product Manager:** Align documentation with feature rollouts and customer communication
- **UX Designer:** Collaborate on user-facing help content and UI microcopy
- **Project Manager:** Ensure documentation is scheduled as part of delivery

### Goals
- Improve user self-service and reduce support tickets
- Accelerate team onboarding and knowledge sharing
- Maintain accurate, up-to-date product information

### Typical Communication
- Documentation PRs and reviews
- Collaboration on release notes and announcements
- Style guides and documentation standards
- Feedback from support and customer-facing teams

---

## Security Engineer

### Role Summary
Security Engineers integrate security best practices into product development by reviewing designs, identifying threats, and validating compliance requirements.

### Responsibilities
- Review architectural and feature proposals for security implications
- Conduct threat modeling and risk assessments
- Guide secure coding practices and code reviews
- Validate compliance with security and regulatory requirements
- Participate in incident response and post-mortems
- Stay current on emerging vulnerabilities and security trends

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers:** Provide secure coding guidance and participate in design reviews
- **Product Manager:** Flag security requirements and compliance needs early in planning
- **Project Manager:** Escalate security blockers; include security reviews in timeline
- **Stakeholders:** Communicate security posture and compliance status

### Goals
- Build security into the product from inception
- Reduce security vulnerabilities and incidents
- Maintain customer trust and regulatory compliance

### Typical Communication
- Threat models and security design reviews
- Code review feedback and security scanning results
- Compliance audit findings
- Incident response communications

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts clarify and refine business requirements, ensuring alignment between business objectives and technical delivery. They map success metrics to business outcomes and support decision-making.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and clarify business requirements from stakeholders
- Translate business needs into user stories and acceptance criteria
- Map success metrics to business KPIs
- Perform impact analysis on proposed changes
- Support sprint planning and backlog refinement
- Contribute to retrospectives to identify process improvements
- Document business rules and domain knowledge

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Manager:** Refine backlog, validate prioritization against business goals
- **Project Manager:** Ensure business requirements are clearly communicated to delivery team
- **Developers:** Clarify acceptance criteria and business context for features
- **Stakeholders:** Serve as liaison between business and delivery teams

### Goals
- Ensure products deliver measurable business value
- Reduce rework caused by unclear requirements
- Improve stakeholder satisfaction and alignment

### Typical Communication
- Requirements documentation and user story refinement
- Business impact analysis and trade-off discussions
- Backlog prioritization meetings
- Retrospective participation and action items

## Cross-Functional Collaboration Model

Successful OctoAcme projects rely on clear communication and active collaboration across roles. Here's how these personas work together:

### Planning Phase
- **Business Analyst** gathers business requirements and maps to success metrics
- **Product Manager** defines goals and prioritizes backlog
- **Project Manager** creates timeline and identifies dependencies
- **Security Engineer** identifies security requirements
- **UX Designer** conducts user research and proposes design approach
- **Technical Writer** plans documentation scope and structure

### Execution Phase
- **Developers** build features with feedback from **UX Designer** and **Security Engineer**
- **Technical Writer** documents features alongside development
- **Project Manager** tracks progress and manages blockers
- **Business Analyst** clarifies requirements and validates alignment

### Review & Release Phase
- **UX Designer** conducts usability testing and design review
- **Security Engineer** validates compliance and performs security review
- **Technical Writer** finalizes documentation and release notes
- **Product Manager** and **Project Manager** coordinate stakeholder communication

### Retrospective & Improvement
- All roles contribute learnings and improvement ideas
- **Project Manager** facilitates; **Business Analyst** maps improvements to business impact
- Action items are tracked and measured
